Notes:

- Extremely hardy perennial onion that can survive severe winters.
- Produces bulbils at the top of the stem instead of flowers.
- The weight of the bulbils causes stems to bend over and root, giving rise to the name "walking onion".
- Virtually every part of the plant is edible: greens, underground bulbs and top bulbils.
- One of the earliest edible crops in spring.
- Can provide harvests throughout the year once established.
- Requires very little maintenance and can persist indefinitely.
- Excellent choice for a perennial vegetable bed.
- Easy to propagate simply by replanting the top bulbils.
- Stronger flavour than Welsh onions but milder than many storage onions.
- Can slowly spread if allowed to "walk" unchecked.
- A good source of onion greens during the hungry gap before most crops are producing.
