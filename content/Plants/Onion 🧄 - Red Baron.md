Notes:

- The most popular red onion variety grown in UK allotments.
- Produces attractive deep red to purple bulbs with red-ringed flesh.
- Renowned for its balance of sweetness and traditional onion flavour.
- Excellent eaten raw in salads, sandwiches and burgers.
- Also performs well when roasted, caramelised or cooked.
- One of the best red onions for pickling.
- Reliable cropper and easy to grow from either seed or sets.
- Stores well for a red onion, though generally not quite as long as the best yellow storage varieties.
- Produces uniform bulbs and performs consistently in UK conditions.
- RHS Award of Garden Merit variety.
- Often recommended as the first red onion variety for beginners.

Why Grow It:

- Adds colour to the harvest basket.
- Excellent for fresh eating.
- One of the best varieties for homemade pickled onions.
- Complements rather than replaces yellow onions.
- Gives a noticeably different flavour profile from Sturon and Yellow Rynsburger.

Comparison:

|Variety|Best Use|
|---|---|
|Sturon|Everyday cooking|
|Yellow Rynsburger|Long-term storage|
|Red Baron|Salads, fresh use, pickling|

If I were growing onions for a household that loves cooking and pickling, Red Baron would be the one red onion I'd insist on including. It's productive, attractive, versatile and reliably performs well in UK gardens.
