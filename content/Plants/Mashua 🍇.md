Notes:

- Traditional Andean tuber crop and one of the "Lost Crops of the Incas".
- Closely related to garden nasturtiums and produces very similar foliage and flowers.
- Grown primarily for its colourful underground tubers.
- Tubers can be white, yellow, orange, red or purple depending on the strain.
- Freshly harvested tubers often have a strong peppery, mustard-like flavour.
- Flavour becomes noticeably sweeter and milder after several weeks of storage.
- Considered one of the most pest-resistant Andean crops.
- Generally suffers from very few diseases and requires little maintenance.
- Produces attractive climbing or scrambling growth throughout summer.
- Both ornamental and productive, making it an interesting addition to an edible garden.
- Requires a long growing season and is usually harvested after the first frosts.
- Can produce several kilograms of tubers from a healthy plant.

suitable for:

- Trellises
- Arches
- Fences
- Wigwams

Container Growing:

- Possible, but not ideal.
- Recommended container size: **40–60 litres minimum**.
- Benefits from support such as a trellis or obelisk.
- Ground growing generally produces better yields.
- Similar container requirements to Tomatillos and Yacon.
