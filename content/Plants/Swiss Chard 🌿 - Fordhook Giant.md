Notes:

- Classic heirloom Swiss chard introduced in the 1930s and still one of the most popular varieties grown today.
- Produces large dark green heavily savoyed leaves with broad white stems.
- One of the highest-yielding chard varieties available.
- Excellent substitute for spinach during summer when spinach tends to bolt.
- Very cold hardy and often continues producing through winter in much of the UK.
- Slow to bolt and tolerant of both heat and cold.
- Leaves can be harvested continuously for many months by picking outer leaves.
- White stems can be cooked separately like celery or asparagus.
- Suitable for raised beds, allotments and large containers.
- RHS Award of Garden Merit variety.

Container Growing:

- Excellent in containers.
- Recommended container size: **20–30 litres**.
- One of the best leafy crops for pots and raised beds.
- Keep well watered for tender leaves.

Why Grow It:

- Extremely productive.
- Very beginner friendly.
- Long harvest season.
- Excellent cold tolerance.
- Cut-and-come-again harvesting.
- One of the best "food per square metre" crops available.

For your allotment, Fordhook Giant is one of those crops that earns its space year after year. A few plants can supply leaves for many months, making it one of the most productive leafy vegetables you've added to your collection.
