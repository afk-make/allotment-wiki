Notes:

- One of the most popular modern parsnip varieties in the UK and a long-standing RHS Award of Garden Merit holder. Produces reliable crops of long, smooth, well-flavoured roots.
- F1 hybrid originally developed by Tozer Seeds and widely regarded as a benchmark parsnip variety.
- Noted for strong resistance to canker, one of the most troublesome diseases affecting parsnips.
- Produces long, slender roots with smooth white skin and vigorous foliage.
- Early bulking variety that can be lifted from autumn onwards.
- Excellent choice for beginners because of its reliability and disease resistance.
- Best grown in deep, stone-free soil to avoid forking of roots.
- Fresh seed should be purchased each year as parsnip seed loses viability quickly.
- Roots become sweeter after exposure to frost and can be left in the ground through winter.
- Stores well in the ground and is ideal for harvesting as required during winter.
- One of the best "grow it and forget it" winter vegetables once established.

For your allotment vault, I'd put **Gladiator** in the same category as **Ben Sarek**, **Gijnlim**, and **French Breakfast**: not necessarily the most unusual choice, but often the variety I'd recommend as the _reference standard_ against which all others are judged.
