30–40 litre container minimum

Notes:

- Traditional Mexican heirloom variety grown for centuries in the milpa system (where maize, beans and squash are grown together).
- Produces attractive purple fruits, often with purple stems and foliage.
- Fruits may remain green inside even when the husk and outer skin develop purple colouring.
- Often regarded as having a richer, more complex flavour than standard green tomatillos.
- Excellent for salsa verde, roasting, sauces and fresh use.
- Highly ornamental and one of the most attractive edible Physalis varieties.
- Requires cross-pollination for the best fruit set; pairing with Toma Verde is an excellent choice.
- Vigorous plants that can become very large and sprawling by late summer.
- Benefits from staking, cages or support, especially when grown in containers.
- Usually crops heavily once established.
- More tolerant of heat and drought than many tomato varieties.
- Harvest when the husks have filled and begin to split.
- A particularly good choice if you want both a productive crop and an eye-catching plant.

If you're growing **one Toma Verde and one Purple de Milpa**, that's actually close to the ideal beginner setup:

|Variety|Colour|Flavour|Pollination|
|---|---|---|---|
|Toma Verde|Green|Classic tangy tomatillo|Pollinates Purple de Milpa|
|Purple de Milpa|Purple|Richer, sweeter flavour|Pollinates Toma Verde|

Together they'll give you better fruit set than a single plant and make a nice comparison in your Obsidian notes for flavour, yield, and suitability to UK conditions.
