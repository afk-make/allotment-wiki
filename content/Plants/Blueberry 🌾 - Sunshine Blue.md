Notes:

- Semi-dwarf Southern Highbush blueberry.
- One of the best blueberry varieties for container growing.
- Naturally compact and tidy compared with most blueberry bushes.
- Produces attractive pink flowers in spring followed by sweet blue berries in summer.
- Self-fertile, so a single plant will produce fruit.
- Cross-pollination with another blueberry variety can improve yields and berry size.
- More tolerant of slightly higher soil pH than many blueberries, though acidic soil is still preferred.
- Semi-evergreen in mild UK winters.
- Attractive ornamental foliage develops burgundy and red tones in autumn.
- Particularly suitable for patios, raised beds and edible landscaping.
- Low chill-hour requirement but still performs well in many parts of the UK.
- Typically reaches around 0.9–1.2 metres tall and wide.

Container Growing:

- Excellent container variety.
- Recommended container size: **30–50 litres**.
- One of the best blueberries for long-term pot culture.
- Use ericaceous compost and rainwater where possible.
- Suitable for patios, courtyards and small gardens.
