Notes:

Acid Soil requirements 

- One of the most widely grown blueberry varieties in the world.
- Considered the benchmark against which many other blueberry varieties are compared.
- Produces large, light-blue berries with good flavour and excellent yields.
- Mid-season cropping, usually following Duke and extending the blueberry harvest season.
- Self-fertile, but benefits significantly from cross-pollination with other varieties.
- Vigorous, upright bush that can become quite large when mature.
- Very reliable and adaptable to a wide range of climates.
- Good disease resistance and generally easy to grow once soil acidity is correct.
- Berries have a good balance of sweetness and acidity, making them suitable for both fresh eating and cooking.
- Excellent choice if you're only growing one blueberry variety, though pairing with Duke or another variety will usually increase yields.
- Requires acidic soil; most UK allotments will need ericaceous compost, containers, or a dedicated acidic bed.
