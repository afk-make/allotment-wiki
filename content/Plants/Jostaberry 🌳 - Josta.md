Notes:

- Hybrid between blackcurrant and gooseberry.
- Produces large dark purple-black berries with a flavour combining characteristics of both parents.
- Thornless, making harvesting much easier than many gooseberry varieties.
- Vigorous grower that can become a substantial bush when mature.
- Self-fertile, so a single plant will produce fruit.
- Generally resistant to many of the diseases that can affect gooseberries and blackcurrants.
- Fruits are excellent for fresh eating, jams, juices, pies and wine.
- Typically crops heavily once established.
- Usually ripens in mid to late summer, often around July.
- Can tolerate a wider range of growing conditions than either parent.
- Often overlooked in favour of gooseberries and currants, but one of the easiest and most productive soft-fruit bushes for a UK allotment.
- Mature bushes can easily reach 1.5–2 m in both height and spread, so allow plenty of room when planning permanent fruit areas.
