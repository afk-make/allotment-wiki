Notes:

- Vigorous climbing plant originating from the Andes of South America.
- Produces masses of hollow green fruits that can be eaten young or stuffed when mature.
- Much more tolerant of cool and wet UK summers than many cucumbers.
- Extremely productive; a single plant can produce dozens of fruits.
- Requires support such as a trellis, fence, arch or wigwam.
- Fruits are best harvested young for eating whole.
- Mature fruits develop large black seeds which are usually removed before cooking.
- Young shoots and leaves are also edible.
- Generally suffers from very few pests and diseases in the UK.
- Frost tender and grown as an annual.
- Can easily reach 3–5 metres of vine growth during a good season.
- Responds well to pruning if it becomes too vigorous.
- One of the easiest unusual climbers to grow and often surprises gardeners with its productivity.
- Excellent choice for covering an arch or trellis while also producing food.

For your database, I think `cultivation_type: indeterminate` is a perfect fit here. Unlike peas or many beans that have a more defined season, achocha just keeps climbing, flowering and fruiting until frost eventually stops it.
