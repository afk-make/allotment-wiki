Notes:

- Traditional Mediterranean perennial grown for its large edible flower buds.
- Harvest buds before they open; once opened they become spectacular purple thistle-like flowers.
- One of the most ornamental edible plants you can grow.
- Produces silver-green architectural foliage throughout much of the year.
- Usually becomes more productive after its second year.
- Individual plants can remain productive for 5–10 years if well maintained.
- Excellent pollinator plant if some buds are allowed to flower.
- Benefits from mulching and winter protection in colder areas of the UK.
- Can be propagated by division of offsets from established plants.
- Buds are harvested from early summer through late summer.
- The edible portion is primarily the fleshy base of the bracts and the heart.
- Makes an excellent feature plant in a perennial vegetable bed.

Space Requirements:

```
1 plant ≈ 1.2 m × 1.2 m
```

This is not a crop to squeeze in. A mature globe artichoke occupies roughly the same footprint as one of your blueberry bushes and should be treated as a long-term perennial.
