Notes:

- One of the most popular commercial and garden asparagus varieties in Europe.
- Selected for high yields and early cropping.
- Produces uniform, thick, high-quality green spears.
- Usually begins cropping slightly earlier than many traditional varieties.
- Can remain productive for 15–20 years or more once established.
- Do not harvest heavily during the first two years after planting crowns; allow plants to build strength.
- After the harvest period, allow the foliage ("ferns") to grow freely through summer to replenish the crowns.
- Excellent resistance to cold weather and generally well suited to UK growing conditions.
- Best planted as one-year-old crowns rather than grown from seed.
- A dedicated asparagus bed is usually worthwhile because the plants occupy the same ground for many years.
- Mature foliage can reach around 1.5 m tall, but because the edible crop is harvested at ground level and the fern growth appears later, I'd still classify it as **🌿 Low Growing** in your system.
- One of the highest-value crops for an allotment because fresh-picked asparagus is dramatically better than supermarket asparagus.
