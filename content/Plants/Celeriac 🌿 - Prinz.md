Notes:

- One of the most popular modern celeriac varieties for allotment growing.
- Produces large, smooth roots with relatively little side-root growth.
- Good resistance to bolting compared with older varieties.
- Reliable performer in UK conditions.
- Benefits from a long growing season and early sowing.
- Requires consistent moisture throughout the season; drought can significantly reduce root size.
- Excellent storage qualities and can often be kept for several months after harvest.
- Particularly well suited to autumn and winter cooking.
- Generally easier to clean and prepare than rougher-rooted varieties.
- Can remain in the ground into winter if conditions allow.
- One of the best celeriac varieties for beginners due to its reliability and root quality.
- The leaves are also edible and can be used similarly to celery leaves for flavouring stocks and soups.

One thing I would note for your category system: celeriac is technically a root crop, but in your layout-based categories it fits nicely into **🌿 Low Growing** because it stays relatively compact, doesn't cast significant shade, and occupies roughly the same physical space as beetroot or lettuce.
