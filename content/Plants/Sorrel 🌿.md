Notes:

- One of the earliest leafy crops to produce usable growth in spring.
- Perennial and extremely reliable once established.
- Harvest young leaves regularly for the best flavour and tenderness.
- Leaves become tougher and more acidic as they age.
- Excellent in soups, sauces, salads and egg dishes.
- Can be harvested repeatedly throughout the growing season.
- Flowers readily in warm weather; removing flower stalks encourages fresh leaf production.
- Tolerates partial shade better than many salad crops.
- Very hardy and usually survives UK winters without protection.
- Forms an attractive clump that gradually increases in size over time.
- Rich in vitamin C and traditionally used as a spring tonic herb.
- Contains oxalic acid, similar to spinach and rhubarb, so it is generally eaten in moderation rather than as a staple vegetable.
- One of the most productive low-maintenance perennial vegetables you can grow on an allotment, especially if you enjoy salads and French-style cooking.
