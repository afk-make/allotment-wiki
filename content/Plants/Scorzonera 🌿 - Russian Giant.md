Notes:

- Traditional variety of black salsify grown for its long black roots and white flesh.
- One of the easiest and most reliable unusual root vegetables for UK allotments.
- Roots can be left in the ground throughout winter and harvested as needed.
- Produces attractive yellow daisy-like flowers if allowed to continue growing into its second year.
- Technically a perennial, though usually grown as a root crop and harvested within the first year.
- Prefers deep, stone-free soil to allow straight root development.
- Less prone to forking than many parsnip varieties.
- Excellent winter vegetable when many other crops are finished.
- Flavour is often compared to a combination of asparagus, parsnip and artichoke.
- Roots can become quite long, often exceeding 30 cm in good soil.
- Very hardy and generally unaffected by frost.
- One of the few root crops that can simply be left in the ground until needed.
- Particularly useful for extending the harvest season into late winter and early spring.

One small note for your database: although the foliage can reach around **120 cm** when flowering, the first-year harvest crop is really a low rosette of leaves. So `category: 🌿 Low Growing` is still the right fit for your allotment-layout system.

One interesting thing for your vault: [[Salsify 🌿]] and Scorzonera make a nice pair of notes because they are frequently confused with one another. If you eventually create Dataview pages by family or crop type, you'll be able to compare them directly:

That's exactly the sort of reference information that's useful years later when you're deciding what unusual crop to try next.
