Notes:

- Thornless cultivar of the traditional Tayberry, making harvesting much easier.
- A hybrid between raspberry and blackberry, producing very large elongated dark red-purple fruits.
- Fruits on second-year canes, similar to summer-fruiting raspberries and blackberries.
- Requires support such as wires, a fence, or a trellis because of its long flexible canes.
- Self-fertile, so only one plant is required for fruit production.
- Very productive and generally considered easier to manage than the original thorny Tayberry.
- Large berries can reach 4–5 cm in length and are excellent for fresh eating, jams, crumbles and freezing.
- Usually harvested from July into August in the UK, although the season can start earlier in warm years.
- Allow plenty of space; mature plants can easily occupy 2 m or more when trained on wires.
- One of the easiest hybrid berries to recommend for an allotment because it combines excellent flavour, heavy crops and thornless canes.
