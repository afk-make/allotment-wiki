Notes:

- South American root crop grown for large sweet storage roots.
- Produces crisp, juicy tubers that are often eaten raw.
- Flavour is frequently compared to apple, pear, melon and water chestnut.
- Related to sunflowers and Jerusalem artichokes rather than potatoes.
- One of the most productive unusual root crops for UK gardens.
- A single mature plant can produce several kilograms of edible roots.
- Harvest after the first frost blackens the foliage.
- Sweetness improves significantly after a few weeks of storage.
- Produces two distinct underground structures:
    - Large storage roots for eating.
    - Crown tubers used for propagation.
- Crown tubers should be saved and replanted the following spring.
- Frost tender above ground but easy to overwinter via stored crowns.
- Attractive sunflower-like foliage makes an impressive feature plant.
- Usually reaches 1.5–2.5 metres tall by late summer.
- One of the easiest perennial-style crops to maintain once you have established planting stock.

Storage & Propagation:


Harvest plant      
↓
Separate storage roots      
↓
Eat / store roots

Separate crown tubers      
↓
Store frost-free      
↓
Replant next spring



Container Growing:

- Possible, but generally not recommended.
- Minimum practical container size: **50–70 litres**.
- Performs much better in open ground.
- Large containers usually reduce yield compared with planting directly into the soil.

Personal Trial Recommendation:

```
1 plant1 m² spaceFull sun
```

Unlike Chinese Artichoke or Malabar Spinach, where I'd suggest a cautious trial, Yacon is one of the unusual crops I'd happily dedicate a full square metre to from the start. The yield-to-effort ratio is often excellent, and it fills a very different niche from Oca, Skirret, Scorzonera and Parsnip.
