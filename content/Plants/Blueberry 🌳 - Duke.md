Notes:

Acid Soil requirements 

- One of the most widely grown early-season blueberry varieties.
- Produces large crops of firm, light-blue berries.
- Excellent frost hardiness despite flowering relatively early.
- Self-fertile, but yields improve significantly when grown with another blueberry variety for cross-pollination.
- Vigorous upright bush that becomes quite substantial when mature.
- Berries store and transport well.
- Often one of the first blueberries to ripen in the UK season.
- Requires acidic soil; most allotment soils will need ericaceous compost, raised beds, or containers.
- Reliable and productive, making it a popular choice for beginners.
- If you're growing multiple blueberries, pairing Duke with another early- or mid-season variety usually improves fruit size and yield.
