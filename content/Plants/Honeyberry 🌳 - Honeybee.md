Notes:

- Primarily grown as an excellent pollination partner for many modern honeyberry varieties, especially Aurora.
- Flowers over a long period and overlaps well with numerous other haskap cultivars.
- Produces edible fruit, but is generally planted for pollination rather than exceptional flavour.
- Very vigorous and reliable grower.
- Extremely cold hardy and tolerant of late frosts.
- Usually flowers early in the season, often before most other soft fruit.
- Can improve yields significantly when planted alongside Aurora and other compatible varieties.
- Fruits are typically smaller and less highly regarded for fresh eating than Aurora.
- Tolerates a wider range of soil conditions than blueberries and does not require acidic soil.
- Birds are very fond of the fruit and may require netting during ripening.
