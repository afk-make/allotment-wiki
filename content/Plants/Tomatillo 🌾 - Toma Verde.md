30–40 litre container minimum

Notes:

- The classic green tomatillo used for salsa verde.
- Produces medium to large green fruits enclosed in papery husks.
- Extremely productive once established; a few plants can yield a surprising amount of fruit.
- Generally easier to grow than tomatoes and less prone to disease problems in the UK.
- Requires cross-pollination with another tomatillo plant for best fruit set.
- Harvest when the husks have filled and begin to split open.
- Fruits can be eaten green and are traditionally harvested before turning yellow.
- Plants often become wider than they are tall and benefit from staking or support.
- Very well suited to large containers (30–40 litres or larger).
- More tolerant of cool UK summers than many people expect.
- Stores well in the husk for several weeks after harvest.
- One of the easiest unusual crops to grow if you enjoy Mexican cooking.
