Notes:

- Ranch is a compact cultivar selected for smaller gardens, typically reaching around 1.2–1.8 m tall and wide, much smaller than standard Autumn Olive which can reach 3–5 m or more.
- Produces masses of small red berries speckled with silver.
- Fruit is rich in lycopene and can be eaten fresh or used in jams, sauces and fruit leather.
- Nitrogen-fixing shrub that can improve soil fertility around it.
- Very tolerant of poor soils, drought and exposed sites once established.
- Flowers are highly fragrant and attractive to pollinators.
- Usually begins fruiting at a relatively young age.
- Can be maintained at around 1.5–2 m with light annual pruning.
- Self-fertile, but fruit production may improve with another Autumn Olive nearby.
- Unlike blueberries, does not require acidic soil and is generally much easier to establish.
- Worth checking local guidance before planting; Autumn Olive is considered invasive in parts of North America, though this is generally less of a concern in the UK.
