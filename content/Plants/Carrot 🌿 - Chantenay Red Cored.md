## 📌 Extra Details


- One of the classic stump-rooted carrot varieties.
- Produces broad-shouldered, conical roots with a deep orange-red core.
- Particularly suited to heavier soils where long-rooted carrots may fork or struggle.
- Typically reaches 12–15 cm in length, shorter than Nantes types.
- Excellent flavour with a high natural sugar content.
- Suitable for fresh eating, roasting, soups and winter storage.
- More tolerant of clay and less-than-perfect soils than many other carrot varieties.
- Popular with allotment growers because it is reliable and forgiving.
- Stores very well when left in the ground or lifted and stored correctly.
- Often considered one of the best carrots for roasting due to its sweetness.
- Direct sowing is strongly preferred; carrots generally dislike transplanting.

Why Grow It:

- Excellent flavour.
- Very reliable.
- Ideal for heavier Worcestershire soils.
- Stores better than many summer carrots.
- Less prone to forking than long-rooted varieties.
- Excellent companion crop for onions and leeks.
