Notes:

- One of the most highly regarded modern honeyberry (haskap) varieties for flavour and fruit quality.
- Produces large elongated berries with excellent sweetness and relatively low bitterness compared with older varieties.
- Usually ripens later than many traditional honeyberry cultivars, helping to extend the harvest season.
- Requires a compatible pollinator variety nearby for good fruit set; honeyberries generally crop much better when grown in pairs or groups.
- Very cold hardy and exceptionally frost tolerant.
- Fruits often ripen before blueberries and around or before the main strawberry season.
- More tolerant of a wider range of soil pH than blueberries and does not require highly acidic soil.
- Birds can strip ripe fruit very quickly, so netting is often worthwhile.
- Can take 3–5 years to reach full productivity and flavour potential.

One thing to note compared with your blueberries: honeyberries are generally much easier to accommodate on an allotment because they tolerate near-neutral soils, whereas blueberries usually require a dedicated acidic bed.
