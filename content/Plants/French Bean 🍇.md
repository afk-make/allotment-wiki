Notes:

- Classic climbing French bean producing long, slender green pods.
- Climbs vigorously and requires a cane, trellis or netting support.
- Far more productive per square metre than dwarf French beans.
- Regular picking encourages continuous flowering and pod production.
- Pods are best harvested young while still tender and stringless.
- Excellent steamed, stir-fried, blanched or frozen.
- One of the highest-yielding vegetables for a small allotment.
- Flowers are attractive to bees and other pollinators.
- Being a legume, it fixes nitrogen in the soil, benefiting following crops.
- Harvest every few days during peak season to keep plants producing.

Support:

- Requires a support approximately **2–2.5 m** tall.
- Traditionally grown on bamboo wigwams or A-frames.
- Can also be trained up netting or wire supports.

Container Growing:

- Suitable for containers.
- Recommended container size: **30 litres minimum**.
- Best grown with a wigwam of canes in a large pot or raised planter.
- Requires frequent watering during flowering and pod production.

Why Grow It:

- Exceptional productivity.
- Long harvesting season.
- Makes excellent use of vertical space.
- Ideal for freezing.
- Great for beginners.
- One of the best crops for maximising harvests from a small allotment.

For your allotment, climbing French beans are a perfect fit. They complement your low-growing root crops by using vertical space, and just a single wigwam of plants can produce an impressive quantity of beans over several months.
