Notes:

- Tropical leafy vegetable grown for its edible leaves and young shoots.
- Despite the name, it is not related to true spinach.
- Produces vigorous climbing vines that can reach 2–4 metres in a warm season.
- Harvest leaves continuously throughout summer and early autumn.
- Best harvested regularly to encourage fresh growth.
- Thrives in hot weather when traditional spinach often bolts or struggles.
- Young leaves are mild and tender; older leaves can develop a slightly mucilaginous texture similar to okra.
- Attractive ornamental plant with glossy leaves and climbing stems.
- Excellent on a trellis, arch, fence, or wigwam.
- Generally more productive in a greenhouse, polytunnel, or against a warm south-facing wall than in open ground.
- Particularly useful for providing leafy greens during midsummer when traditional spinach is difficult to grow.

Container Growing:

- Excellent candidate for container growing.
- Recommended container size: **20–30 litres**.
- A large pot combined with a trellis or obelisk works very well.
- Often performs better in a container placed in a warm, sheltered position than in open ground.
- Ideal for patios, greenhouses, and sunny sheltered corners of the allotment.

Comparison Notes:

- Similar growing habit to **[[Achocha 🍇]]** and **[[Cucamelon 🍇]]**, though grown for leaves rather than fruits.
- Fills a useful summer leafy-green niche between spring crops like **[[Sorrel]]** and autumn/winter crops such as **[[Scorzonera 🌿 - Russian Giant]]** and **[[Parsnip 🌿 - Gladiator]]**.
- One of the few leafy vegetables that genuinely prefers the hottest part of the growing season.
