Notes:

- One of the most popular perennial Welsh onion varieties in the UK.
- Forms expanding clumps rather than producing large bulbs.
- Extremely hardy and reliably overwinters without protection.
- Can provide harvests throughout much of the year, especially during mild UK winters.
- Harvest individual stems as needed or divide mature clumps every few years to create new plants.
- Excellent substitute for spring onions in cooking.
- Produces attractive globe-shaped white flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators.
- One of the easiest perennial vegetables to grow and maintain.
- Particularly valuable during the "hungry gap" when annual vegetables are scarce.
- Can be harvested almost continuously once established.
- Older clumps become increasingly productive and can persist for many years.
- A very good companion for a perennial vegetable bed alongside sorrel, asparagus, chives and garlic chives.
One small thought on your schema: this is a good example where the `harvest_from: Jan` / `harvest_to: Dec` values are genuinely appropriate. Unlike most crops, Evergreen Hardy White can be harvested in some form virtually all year once established, especially if you're taking stems selectively rather than lifting entire plants.
