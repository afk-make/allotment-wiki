Notes:

- Classic perennial herb grown for its hollow onion-flavoured leaves.
- One of the easiest perennial edibles to grow in the UK.
- Forms neat clumps that slowly increase in size over time.
- Excellent companion plant throughout the vegetable garden.
- Produces attractive purple pom-pom flowers in late spring and early summer.
- Flowers are edible and highly attractive to bees and other pollinators.
- Leaves can be harvested repeatedly from spring until autumn.
- Best harvested by cutting whole leaves near the base rather than trimming the tips.
- Benefits from dividing every few years to maintain vigour.
- Very winter hardy and usually among the earliest perennial herbs to reappear in spring.
- Ideal for edging perennial vegetable beds due to its compact growth habit.
- One of the few crops that combines culinary use, pollinator value and ornamental appeal.
