Notes:

- Traditional root vegetable once popular in Victorian kitchens.
- Often called "Oyster Plant" because some people detect a mild oyster-like flavour.
- Produces long white roots with creamy flesh.
- Generally considered more difficult to grow than Scorzonera because roots are more prone to forking.
- Requires deep, stone-free soil for best root development.
- Can be left in the ground through winter and lifted as required.
- Attractive purple flowers appear if plants are allowed to bolt or overwinter.
- Young shoots, flower buds and roots are all edible.
- Excellent roasted, mashed, steamed or added to soups and stews.
- Less commonly grown than Scorzonera but historically more famous.
- A useful crop for extending the winter harvest season.
- Particularly valued by gardeners interested in heritage and forgotten vegetables.

One interesting thing for your vault: Salsify and [[Scorzonera 🌿 - Russian Giant|Scorzonera/Black Salsify]] make a nice pair of notes because they are frequently confused with one another. If you eventually create Dataview pages by family or crop type, you'll be able to compare them directly:
