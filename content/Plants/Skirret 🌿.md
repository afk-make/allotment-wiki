Notes:

- Traditional European root crop that was widely grown before potatoes became common.
- Produces clusters of long white roots rather than a single large root.
- Exceptionally sweet flavour, often compared to parsnip with hints of carrot and chestnut.
- One of the few perennial root vegetables.
- Can remain in the ground for several years and harvested as needed.
- Usually propagated by dividing crowns rather than by seed.
- Extremely hardy and well suited to UK conditions.
- Harvested mainly through autumn and winter when sweetness is at its best.
- Produces attractive white umbel flowers that are popular with pollinators.
- Roots can contain a woody central core in older plants, which is usually removed before cooking.
- Less productive per square metre than potatoes but far easier to maintain once established.
- One of the most commonly recommended "forgotten vegetables" because it combines good flavour with easy cultivation.
- Particularly valuable in a perennial vegetable bed alongside asparagus, sorrel, Welsh onions and walking onions.

A small observation for your database: Skirret is one of those rare crops where the values

```
perennial: true cultivation_type: winter-harvest
```

tell you almost everything important at a glance. There are very few vegetables that are both perennial and primarily harvested in winter, which makes it a useful addition if you're interested in year-round production.
