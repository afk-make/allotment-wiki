## 📌 Extra Details


- Traditional heirloom turnip dating back to the 19th century.
- Produces smooth, round golden-yellow roots with yellow flesh.
- Considerably sweeter and milder than many white turnips.
- Excellent roasted, mashed, steamed or added to soups and stews.
- Can be eaten raw when harvested young.
- Both roots and leaves are edible.
- Fast growing and suitable for succession sowing throughout the season.
- Particularly well suited to autumn production.
- Reliable crop in UK conditions.
- One of the best turnips for gardeners who think they don't like turnips.

Container Growing:

- Suitable for containers.
- Recommended container size: **15–20 litres minimum**.
- Performs well in raised beds and deep planters.
- Requires consistent watering to maintain tenderness.

Why Grow It:

- Fast harvest.
- Sweet flavour.
- Easy to grow.
- Excellent roasted.
- Good autumn crop.
- Suitable for containers and raised beds.

For your collection, I'd classify Golden Ball as one of those "surprisingly good" traditional vegetables. Unlike some stronger-flavoured turnips, it has a mild, buttery sweetness that makes it much easier to enjoy in everyday cooking. It also fits nicely into your succession-sowing approach because you can sow small batches from spring right through summer.
