## 📌 Extra Details



Notes:

- One of the largest kohlrabi varieties available.
- Famous for producing enormous swollen stems while remaining tender.
- Unlike most varieties, it can grow to **2–5 kg** without becoming woody.
- Produces pale green bulbs with excellent flavour.
- Sweet, juicy flesh similar to broccoli stem with a hint of cabbage.
- Excellent eaten raw, roasted, steamed or mashed.
- Young leaves are also edible and can be cooked like spring greens.
- Slower growing than standard kohlrabi but rewards patience with huge harvests.
- Extremely forgiving if harvest is delayed.
- Excellent conversation piece on an allotment because of its unusual appearance and size.

Container Growing:

- Suitable for containers.
- Recommended container size: **20–30 litres**.
- Performs best in raised beds or open ground where very large bulbs can develop.
- Keep well watered for the sweetest, most tender flesh.
