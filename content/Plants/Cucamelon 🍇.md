Notes:

- Produces tiny fruits resembling miniature watermelons, typically 2–4 cm long.
- Native to Central America and considerably more tolerant of cool weather than many cucumbers.
- Extremely productive once established, often yielding hundreds of fruits from a single plant.
- Fruits are best harvested young and eaten fresh, pickled, or added to salads.
- The flavour is similar to cucumber with a hint of lime or citrus.
- Requires support such as a trellis, netting, wigwam, or arch.
- Generally healthier and less troublesome than conventional cucumbers.
- Can be grown successfully in containers, greenhouses, or open ground.
- Produces small underground tubers that can sometimes be overwintered and replanted the following year.
- Continues flowering and fruiting until the first frosts.
- Excellent choice for children and visitors because of the unusual miniature fruits.
- One of the easiest unusual climbers to grow in the UK.
- Often crops more reliably outdoors than standard cucumbers in cooler summers.
