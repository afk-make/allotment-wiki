Notes:

Acid Soil requirements 

- An early-season highbush blueberry variety, often ripening around the same time as Duke.
- Produces very large berries, often larger than those of Duke and Bluecrop.
- Excellent cold hardiness and one of the toughest blueberry varieties available.
- More tolerant of heavier or less-than-perfect soils than many blueberries, though it still requires acidic conditions.
- Compact, upright growth habit makes it well suited to allotments and smaller gardens.
- Self-fertile, but crops more heavily when grown with other blueberry varieties.
- Reliable and productive once established.
- Particularly valued for fruit size and vigorous growth.
- Good disease resistance and generally considered one of the easier blueberry varieties for beginners.
- A useful companion to Duke and Bluecrop because it helps improve cross-pollination and extends the productivity of a blueberry patch.
- Like all blueberries, performs best in ericaceous compost or a dedicated acidic bed.
