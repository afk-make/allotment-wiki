One thing worth noting for your planning: unlike radishes, these **do require three spaces at the same time**. All three varieties occupy the ground from late spring until winter. The benefit isn't space efficiency—it's extending your harvest season by about **five months** from October through February.

If your family genuinely eats a lot of sprouts, a row of each variety would be a very sensible use of space. A dozen plants of each could easily keep a sprout-loving household supplied through much of the winter.
