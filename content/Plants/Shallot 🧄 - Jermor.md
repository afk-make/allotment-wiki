Notes:

- Widely regarded as one of the finest French shallot varieties.
- Produces long, copper-pink bulbs with excellent flavour.
- Favoured by chefs for its balance of sweetness and complexity.
- Milder and more refined than most storage onions.
- Excellent for sauces, vinaigrettes, roasting and French cuisine.
- Forms clusters of multiple bulbs from a single planted set.
- Usually produces 6–12 daughter bulbs per planted bulb.
- Stores exceptionally well when properly cured.
- One of the most reliable shallots for UK growing conditions.
- Typically grown from sets rather than seed.
- High yielding and easy to grow.
- RHS Award of Garden Merit variety.

Propagation:

- One of the easiest alliums to save and replant.
- Simply retain some of the harvested bulbs each year.
- Replant the best bulbs the following spring.
- Can effectively become a self-sustaining crop.

Why Grow It:

- Superior flavour for cooking.
- Excellent storage.
- Easy propagation.
- Highly productive from a small area.
- One of the best-value alliums you can dedicate space to.

If your dad enjoys cooking, Jermor may end up becoming his favourite allium. Many gardeners find that once they start growing good shallots, they reach for them in the kitchen almost as often as onions because the flavour is richer and more complex while still being easy to grow and store.
