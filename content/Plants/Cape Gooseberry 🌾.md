Notes:

- Closely related to tomatillos but grown for its sweet fruit rather than savoury uses.
- Produces bright orange berries enclosed in decorative papery husks.
- Self-fertile; a single plant can produce a substantial crop.
- Particularly well suited to container growing and greenhouse cultivation.
- Usually fruits from late summer until the first frosts.
- Ripe fruits often drop naturally from the plant and can be collected from the ground.
- Flavour develops significantly as fruits fully ripen inside their husks.
- Can be overwintered in a frost-free greenhouse or conservatory, where it may behave as a short-lived perennial.
- More tolerant of cool UK summers than many tropical fruits.
- Benefits from support or staking once heavily laden with fruit.
- Attractive ornamental plant as well as a productive crop.
- One of the easiest exotic-tasting fruits to grow in the UK.
- Often performs better in a large container or greenhouse than in open ground.

A couple of comments on the properties:

- I would keep `perennial: false` because that's how most UK allotment growers treat it, even though it is technically a perennial in frost-free climates.
- `cultivation_type: indeterminate` fits reasonably well because, like tomatillos and many tomatoes, it continues producing flowers and fruit until stopped by cold weather rather than cropping in one flush.
