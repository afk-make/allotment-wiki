Notes:

- One of the easiest and most useful culinary herbs to grow.
- Evergreen in most UK winters, allowing harvests throughout the year.
- Extremely attractive to bees and other pollinators when flowering.
- Thrives in poor, dry soils where many other plants struggle.
- Dislikes waterlogged ground and excessive feeding.
- Excellent companion plant for brassicas because its scent may help confuse some pests.
- Can be grown successfully in containers, raised beds, herb spirals, and gravel gardens.
- Benefits from a light trim after flowering to keep plants compact.
- Leaves retain flavour well when dried.
- Particularly useful on an allotment because it is both ornamental and productive.
- One plant can remain productive for several years before becoming woody.

because they share very similar "plant once, harvest for years" characteristics to the perennial vegetables you're already planning.
