Notes:

- One of the most widely grown modern redcurrant varieties.
- Produces exceptionally long trusses of bright red berries.
- Renowned for heavy crops and reliable performance.
- Self-fertile and does not require a pollination partner.
- One mature bush can produce several kilograms of fruit annually.
- Berries hang well on the plant and can often be harvested over an extended period.
- Excellent disease resistance and strong vigour.
- Particularly well suited to UK gardens and allotments.
- Fruit is ideal for jams, jellies, sauces, desserts and summer puddings.
- Sharp when eaten fresh but sweetens noticeably when fully ripe.
- Long fruit strings make harvesting quicker and easier than many older varieties.
- RHS Award of Garden Merit variety.

Container Growing:

- Possible in large containers.
- Recommended container size: **40–50 litres minimum**.
- Performs best in open ground.
- Requires regular watering if container grown.
