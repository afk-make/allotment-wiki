Notes:

- Classic Nantes-type summer carrot and one of the most widely grown carrot varieties.
- Produces smooth cylindrical roots with blunt ends and very little core.
- Renowned for its sweet flavour and crisp, tender texture.
- Typically reaches 15–20 cm in length.
- Excellent for fresh eating, salads, juicing and cooking.
- Particularly well suited to raised beds and lighter soils.
- More tolerant of imperfect soil conditions than many long-rooted carrot varieties.
- Direct sowing is strongly preferred; carrots generally dislike transplanting.
- Successional sowing from spring into summer provides a long harvest season.
- One of the best beginner carrot varieties.
- Stores reasonably well but is primarily considered a summer carrot rather than a dedicated winter storage carrot.
- Virtually coreless, making it sweeter and more tender than many older carrot types.
