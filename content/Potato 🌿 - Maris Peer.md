## 📌 Extra Details

Notes:

- Classic British second early potato introduced in the 1960s.
- Produces small to medium, round to oval tubers with smooth pale yellow skin and creamy flesh.
- One of the finest salad potatoes available.
- Firm, waxy texture that holds together beautifully after cooking.
- Excellent for boiling, steaming, potato salads and eating hot with butter.
- High yielding and quick to mature.
- Suitable for growing in containers, potato bags and raised beds.
- Can be harvested as tender "new potatoes" around 12–14 weeks after planting.
- Good resistance to common scab and dry rot.
- RHS Award of Garden Merit variety.

Container Growing:

- Excellent for containers.
- Recommended container size: **30 litres minimum**.
- Plant **2 seed potatoes** per 30-litre container.
- Keep evenly watered during tuber formation.
- Ideal for patio growing and autumn second-crop experiments.

Why Grow It:

- Excellent flavour.
- Quick to mature.
- Superb waxy texture.
- Perfect for summer meals.
- Excellent in containers.
- One of the best varieties for harvesting as classic British "new potatoes."

For your collection, **Maris Peer** and **Charlotte** complement each other nicely rather than competing. Maris Peer is the earlier of the two and excels as a traditional new potato, while Charlotte offers slightly larger tubers with a richer, nuttier flavour and greater versatility. Together with Sarpo Mira, you'd have an excellent spread of potatoes from early summer right through to the following spring.
