2 rows in [[Large Bed E - Beans and Peas]]

### 1. Build the support first

- Push 2.4 m (8 ft) bamboo canes into the ground.
- Space the canes about **30 cm apart**.
- Lean them together to make an A-frame or wigwam along the row.
- Tie a long horizontal cane across the top to stop them moving.

### 2. Plant spacing

Your seedlings are quite small, so don't crowd them.

- **15–20 cm between plants** along each row.
- **20–25 cm between the two rows.**

That works out at roughly **10–12 plants per metre** of bed.

### 3. Planting

The plants have been sitting in small pots, so:

- Water the pots first.
- Plant them at the same depth they were growing.
- Water each one in thoroughly.
- Gently wind the shoots onto the canes—they'll soon grab on themselves.

### 4. Feed

Beans don't need lots of nitrogen.

Instead:

- Mix in a little garden compost if you have it.
- Once flowers start opening, give a weekly **tomato feed** (high potash).

### 5. Mulch

A 5 cm layer of compost, leaf mould or grass clippings around the plants will help keep the soil moist.

Pick pods as soon as they are a good size. Don't let them stay on too long, the plant will stop producing new flowers.

## Looking at your seedlings

Some are much greener than others.

I'd plant **every one of them**. Even the pale ones should recover once their roots reach open soil. I wouldn't discard any unless they're completely dead.

### One tip

The yellow leaves probably won't become green again. Watch the **new growth** over the next 1–2 weeks. If the new leaves come out a healthy deep green, you'll know the problem was simply that they had exhausted the compost in their pots.

Given how many plants you have, you should end up with a very productive row. I suspect you'll have enough for regular picking through August and into September if you keep harvesting the beans while they're young.
