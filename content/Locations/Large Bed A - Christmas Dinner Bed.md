It's not only practical—it's one of the nicest things about a UK allotment.

The trick is to focus on crops that either:

1. **Stand happily in the ground through winter**, or
2. **Can be harvested and stored** until Christmas.

For a Christmas harvest, you're really gardening for **December**, not autumn.

## Crops you can sow in July and harvest around Christmas




### Root crops

#### Carrot

Your [[Carrot 🌿 - Nantes 2]] and [[Carrot 🌿 - Chantenay Red Cored]] plan is perfect.

- July sowing.
- Harvest from October onwards.
- Can often stay in the ground until Christmas in much of the UK.

#### Beetroot

Especially Detroit 2 Beetroot.

- July sowing.
- Harvest through autumn.
- Many can be left in the ground into winter.

#### Black Spanish Round Radish

Almost designed for this purpose.

- Sow July–August.
- Harvest from late autumn through winter.
- Excellent keeper.

#### Turnip

- Quick growing.
- Good for winter meals.

---

## Leaf crops

#### Swiss Chard

One of the best winter crops.

- Sow July.
- Harvest leaves until hard frosts.
- Often survives mild UK winters.

#### Spinach

- July/August sowing.
- Autumn and winter leaves.

---

## Brassicas for Christmas

#### Spring Cabbage

Not for Christmas dinner itself, but useful through winter.

#### Brussels Sprouts

The classic Christmas crop.

However, these need to be started much earlier (March–April), not July.

#### Kale

Another excellent Christmas harvest crop, though usually sown or planted earlier than July.

---

## Potatoes for Christmas

#### Potato

You can grow "Christmas potatoes".

- Plant cold-stored seed potatoes in late July or August.
- Use large pots or grow bags.
- Protect from frost.
- Harvest around December.

This is a bit of a novelty but can be great fun.

## The allotment Christmas dinner challenge

If I were designing a bed now specifically so your dad could harvest food on Christmas Eve, I'd sow:

|Crop|Sow|
|---|---|
|Nantes 2 carrot|July|
|Chantenay carrot|July|
|Detroit 2 beetroot|July|
|Black Spanish radish|July|
|Swiss chard|July|
|Spinach|July/August|

And if you're willing to buy plants rather than sow:

- Leek (absolutely fantastic at Christmas)
- Kale

## The most "Christmas Day" crops

For a family meal, I'd prioritise:

1. Leeks
2. Carrots
3. Brussels sprouts
4. Kale
5. Beetroot
6. Christmas potatoes

Imagine walking down to the allotment on Christmas Eve and coming back with:

- Fresh carrots
- Leeks
- Kale
- Beetroot
- A few Black Spanish radishes for a winter salad

That's very achievable in most parts of the UK with planning from July onward. In fact, the carrots, beetroot, and winter radishes you're already considering are exactly the sort of crops that make it possible.
