### What to do with the last metre

You have a few good options:

1. **More carrots (my favourite)** – sow another block of Nantes 5 for extra winter carrots.
2. **Another sowing of radishes** – you'll have a second crop ready after the first.
3. **Leave it empty for now** – sow it in late July or August with winter crops such as spinach, pak choi, mizuna, lamb's lettuce or more turnips.

I'd actually choose **option 3**. One of the biggest lessons with allotments is not to sow everything at once. Keeping some space free lets you keep harvesting right through autumn instead of having a glut all at the same time.

Since I know you're building a long-term allotment with several permanent beds, we can also start thinking in terms of **crop rotation**. For example:

- **Bed A** – Roots (carrots, beetroot, radish, turnips)
- **Bed B** – Brassicas
- **Bed C** – Legumes (beans and peas)
- **Bed D** – Potatoes
- **Bed E** – Onions and leeks

Then each year, each crop group moves to the next bed. That helps reduce pests and diseases and keeps the soil healthier over time. It would fit very nicely with the bed names you've already created in your Obsidian vault.
