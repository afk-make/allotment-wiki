
## Main Cooking Onion

### [[Onion 🧄 - Setton]]

[[Onion 🧄 - Sturon]] 2nd choice

This would be my first choice today.

- Excellent flavour
- Heavy yields
- Very reliable
- Stores extremely well
- Improved version of Sturon

For curries, stews, sauces and everyday cooking, this would probably become the workhorse onion.

---

## Long Storage Onion

### Yellow Rynsburger

One of the classic UK storage onions.

- Harvest late summer
- Stores through winter
- Dense bulbs
- Excellent keeper

If your dad cooks with onions constantly, a storage onion is invaluable.

---

## Red Onion

### Red Baron

Probably the easiest recommendation of all.

- Beautiful colour
- Good flavour
- Great for salads
- Excellent for pickling
- Stores well for a red onion

I wouldn't build an onion collection without Red Baron.

---

## Pickling Onion

### Silverskin

For proper pickled onions.

These are grown densely to produce small bulbs ideal for jars.

If your dad makes a lot of pickled onions, I'd dedicate a short row to these every year.

---

## Early Onion

### Radar

Plant in autumn.

- Harvest very early
- Reliable overwintering variety
- Gives onions before spring-sown onions are ready

This fills the hungry gap nicely.

A small clump of a good shallot variety such as Jermor would fit beautifully into Dad's Onion Collection and give him something quite different from the bulb onions, Welsh onions and walking onions you're already planning.
