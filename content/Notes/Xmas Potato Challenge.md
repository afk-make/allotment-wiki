
For "Christmas potatoes" grown in containers, you're looking for varieties that:

- Grow quickly.
- Tuber up reliably in pots.
- Are commonly available as cold-stored seed potatoes in summer.
- Taste great when harvested young.

### My top recommendation: Charlotte Potato

- Excellent flavour.
- Waxy salad potato.
- Very reliable in containers.
- Produces lots of small-to-medium tubers.
- Fantastic freshly harvested at Christmas.

Many gardeners consider Charlotte one of the best-tasting potatoes you can grow.

### Other excellent choices

#### Nicola Potato

- Similar to Charlotte.
- Great flavour.
- Reliable in bags and pots.

#### Maris Peer Potato

- Classic salad potato.
- Good for Christmas harvests.
- Produces well in containers.

#### Jazzy Potato

- Lots of small potatoes.
- Excellent container performance.
- Fun if you like baby potatoes.

### Container setup

For a Christmas crop:

- Plant in late July to early August.
- Use a 40–50 litre pot or grow bag.
- Good multipurpose compost mixed with some garden soil.
- Keep watered consistently.
- Move under cover or protect from hard frosts in late autumn.

### Manage expectations

Christmas potatoes are more of a novelty crop than a staple.

For a family of two, one or two large containers can easily provide enough fresh potatoes for Christmas dinner, but you're not going to get the sort of yield you'd expect from a spring-planted maincrop.

### If it were my allotment

For maximum fun and the best chance of success, I'd plant:

1. **Charlotte** (first choice)
2. **Maris Peer** (second choice)

Then on Christmas Eve you'd have:

- Fresh-dug carrots
- Leeks
- Turnips
- Chard
- And a pot of freshly tipped-out Charlotte potatoes

That's about as good as allotment gardening gets in winter.
